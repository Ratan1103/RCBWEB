ABOUT    THE    FRANCHISE

Royal Challengers Bangalore (often abbreviated as RCB) are a franchise cricket team
based in Bangalore, Karnataka, that plays in the Indian Premier League (IPL). It was founded in 2008
by United Spirits and named after the company's liquor brand Royal Challenge.
Since its inception, the team has played its home matches at the
M. Chinnaswamy Stadium.

In September 2007, the Board of Control for Cricket in India(BCCI) announced the
establishment of the Indian Premier League, a Twenty20 competition to be started in 2008.
The teams for the competition, representing 8 different cities of India, including Bangalore,
were put up on auction in Mumbai on 20 February 2008. The Bangalore franchise was purchased by Vijay Mallya,
who paid US$111.6 million for it. This was the second highest bid for a team, next only to Reliance Industries'
bid of US$111.9 million for the Mumbai Indians. The brand value of Royal Challengers Bangalore
was estimated to be ₹595 crore (US$75 million) in 2019.


FANBASE

Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) has the most loyal
fanbase than any other cricket franchise in IPL
or any other cricket tournament. From January 1 to
December 31, 2021, RCB ranked in the eighth
position in terms of the highest fan engagement
by sports clubs in the world. RCB has beaten the teams like
Mumbai Indians and Chennai Super Kings, who are the most successful teams
in the IPL. CSK is the only other team that features in the top ten teams
in terms of fan engagement. The top five positions have been taken by
European football clubs like Manchester United, FC Barcelona, Real Madrid,
Paris Saint Germain, and Chelsea FC. RCB and CSK are the only
two cricket clubs in the top ten list.
